Some other Covid symptoms to look out for:

  • Most common symptoms
    • dry cough
    • tiredness
  • Less common symtoms
    • aches and pains
    • diarrhea
    • conjunctivitis
    • loss of taste or smell
    • a rash on skin, or discoloration of fingers or toes
  • Serious symptoms
    • difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
    • chest pain or pressure
    • loss of speech or movement

It is important to note that some individuals are more at risk of developing more life-threatening symptoms.

They may fall under one or more of the following categories:

  • Aged over 60 and above
  • High blood pressure
  • Heart problems/disease
  • Lung problems/disease
  • Diabetes
  • Cancer
  • Obesity
  • Asthma

Incase any serious symptoms develop seek medical attention. It is always recommended to contact the health facility before hand and inform them before a visit so that they may prepare or redirect you to the right healthcare facility.

Individuals with mild symptoms should stay home and quarantine. Keep tracking the severity of the symptoms and inform local medical personnel through the means made possible by local government programs.

It takes an average of 5-6 days for infected individuals to show any symptoms if they have been infected by the virus, however it can take up to 14 days in some individuals. Some may be infected and may not exhibit symptoms.
